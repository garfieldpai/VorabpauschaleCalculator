What is the Vorabpauschale?

For investment funds that don't distribute all their income each year (or are domiciled outside Germany and have no German custodian to withhold tax automatically), German tax law deems a minimum return to have been received — even if you haven't sold anything. This deemed amount is the Vorabpauschale ("advance lump sum"). It's taxed in the year it accrues, on the same basis as other capital income.

§18 Abs. 1 InvStG: "Die Vorabpauschale ist der Betrag, um den die Ausschüttungen eines Investmentfonds innerhalb eines Kalenderjahres den Basisertrag für dieses Kalenderjahr unterschreiten..."

The formula, step by step

1. Basisertrag (deemed minimum return)

Basisertrag = NAV at start of year × Basiszins × 70%

The NAV used is the fund's value on 1 January — the same figure for every unit, regardless of when during the year you actually bought it.

2. Mehrbetrag (the cap)

Mehrbetrag = (NAV at year end − NAV at year start) + distributions per unit

The Basisertrag can never exceed this — you're never deemed to have earned more than the fund actually returned. If the fund lost value, the Mehrbetrag is capped at zero.

3. Acquisition-year reduction (for SIP / mid-year purchases)

Units bought during the year get a reduced Vorabpauschale: 1/12 less for each full month before the purchase month. A unit bought in August keeps 5/12 of the full-year amount (Aug, Sep, Oct, Nov, Dec).

4. Rounding — only once, at the very end

Per the BMF's 21 May 2019 circular (para 18.4), the per-unit Basisertrag must be kept to at least 4 decimal places. Rounding to cents happens only after multiplying by the total units held on 31 December — never before.

"Der Basisertrag ist mit mindestens vier Nachkommastellen anzusetzen und erst nach der Multiplikation mit der Anzahl der... Anteile... ist eine kaufmännische Rundung auf zwei Nachkommastellen vorzunehmen."
5. Currency conversion

The Vorabpauschale is deemed received on the first business day of the following year. This tool converts the final native-currency total to EUR using the exchange rate on 31 December of the relevant year — once, at the end, not per tranche.

6. Teilfreistellung (partial exemption) — verify before relying on it

German/EU-regulated equity funds get a 30% exemption. Whether a given foreign fund (e.g. one regulated by SEBI in India, or another non-EU regulator) qualifies as an "Investmentfonds" under §1 InvStG at all — and therefore for this exemption — is fact-specific and not assumed automatically by this tool. Leave the Teilfreistellung toggle off unless you've confirmed the fund's qualification, ideally with a Steuerberater.
